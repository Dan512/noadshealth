## Design Context

### Users

General adults — not athletes, not patients, just people trying to be healthier. Often skeptical of fitness/health marketing, which is why they're on a site whose name *is* its anti-ads stance. Read primarily on mobile (phone in bed, waiting rooms, transit) but also desktop. They're looking for honest, practical guidance from someone who clearly knows what they're talking about and won't try to sell them a course.

### Brand Personality

**Three words**: plain-spoken, practical, opinionated.

The voice is a knowledgeable friend who lifts and reads the research. Doesn't soften claims to please everyone. Doesn't hedge with "consult your doctor" boilerplate when a direct answer is fine. Says "the WHO cutoffs" not "studies have shown." Comfortable being slightly contrarian when the consensus is wrong.

**What this is NOT** (explicit anti-references):

- **Influencer / supplement-brand marketing**. No hype, no exclamation points, no muscle-fit photography, no "you got this," no countdown urgency.
- **Sterile medical establishment** (WebMD, Mayo Clinic). No dense legal disclaimers, no blue-corporate UI, no clip-art doctors, no breadcrumb-heavy navigation.
- **Wellness woo / mystical / Goop-adjacent**. No soft pastels, no "clean living" aesthetic, no incense-and-crystals palette, no pseudoscience-adjacent visual cues.
- **Bro-forum / Reddit r/Fitness density**. No walls of unformatted text, no gatekeepy tone, no locker-room voice.

### Aesthetic Direction

**Primary reference**: a well-thumbed field guide or handbook. Utilitarian but *considered*. Things have specific places — readers trust the layout because it's consistent. Body type is optimized for actual sustained reading. Sturdy in a way that signals durability over trendiness. Authority through clarity, not through cold sterility.

**Theme**: Light + dark, default to system preference. Light is the primary writing surface (handbook = printed page); dark exists for late-night reading.

**Typography** (hard constraint: system fonts only — no Google Fonts, no remote loads):

- Distinctive serif for headlines from the system stack — currently Georgia, with room to push into Iowan Old Style / Charter on better-equipped platforms via stack.
- Comfortable sans for body from the system stack (-apple-system, Segoe UI, Roboto, etc.).
- 5-step modular scale, at least 1.25 ratio between steps.
- Line length capped ~65-75ch. Line height generous on body (~1.65), tighter on headlines.

**Color**:

- Warm near-white `#fafaf7` and warm near-black `#0c0c0e` for surfaces. Tint everything toward the brand evergreen — even neutrals carry a hint.
- Deep evergreen `#2c5f2d` (light) / sage `#7cb87f` (dark) as the single accent. Used in the "ads" wordmark span and interactive hover states. Sparingly.
- Coral `#ff5e5b` is brand-fixed across all NoAdsDude properties for the Ko-fi support button. Never used for anything else.
- High-contrast monochrome dominates. Body text must hit WCAG AAA in both themes (operator is colorblind).
- Avoid red/green-only signal distinctions anywhere in the UI.

### Design Principles

1. **Reading first.** Body type, line length, leading, paragraph spacing — all calibrated for sustained reading on phone and laptop. If a visual flourish hurts readability, it loses.
2. **Authority through clarity.** No "as a health expert" framing, no credentials, no warning boxes, no badges. Confidence comes from the text being correct, the layout being predictable, and the design being unhurried.
3. **Structural hierarchy, restrained color.** Hierarchy lives in type weight, size, and white space — not in color blocks. The single accent (evergreen) is reserved for genuine emphasis and interactivity.
4. **Brand-fixed elements are sacred.** Ko-fi coral, the "no/ads/health" wordmark color split, zero third-party scripts on page load, AAA body-text contrast, mobile-first — these never get negotiated for aesthetic reasons.
5. **Sturdy over trendy.** A 1985 field guide still reads well. A 2026 fitness brand probably won't in five years. When in doubt, choose the option that will age well.
